  6. Section: "How AI Optimization Reduces Changeovers" -- the optimizer evaluates thousands of sequences, finds groupings humans miss, balances changeover reduction against other objectives
  7. Section: "Measuring Improvement" -- total changeover time per shift, changeover-to-processing ratio, number of changeovers per resource
  8. Callout type="info": Changeover cost calculation example
  9. Callout type="tip": Start by measuring current changeover time before optimizing
  10. CTA: Link to /en/roi-calculator and /en/contact

- Title: "AI Scheduling vs Manual Planning: What Manufacturers Need to Know"
- Description: 150-160 chars, include "ai scheduling vs manual planning"
- Date: "2026-02-10"
- Tags: ["ai", "optimization", "scheduling"]
- Target: 1,200-1,800 words
- Content structure:
  1. Opening -- the debate is not "AI replaces planners" but "AI amplifies planners"
  2. Section: "How Manual Planning Works Today" -- describe the typical process (planner opens Excel, looks at order list, places orders by experience, adjusts for conflicts by feel)
  3. Section: "Where Manual Planning Falls Short" -- combinatorial limits (20 machines x 100 orders = millions of possible schedules), bias toward familiar patterns, slow reaction to disruptions, knowledge loss when planner leaves
  4. Section: "What AI Scheduling Actually Does" -- define it clearly: optimizer evaluates many schedules against objectives (minimize lateness, reduce changeovers, balance load), returns the best one found. NOT fully autonomous -- planner sets objectives and constraints
  5. Comparison table: Manual Planning vs AI Scheduling across dimensions (speed, optimality, disruption handling, knowledge retention, setup effort, cost)
  6. Section: "The Hybrid Approach" -- AI generates the schedule, planner reviews and adjusts. AI handles math, planner handles context (strategic customers, shop floor realities, upcoming maintenance)
  7. Section: "When to Consider AI Scheduling" -- signals: scheduling takes >2 hours/week, frequent late orders, more than 10 resources, complex routing
  8. Callout type="info": Statistic about scheduling time reduction (70-80% from ai-in-manufacturing post)
  9. Callout type="warning": Common misconception that AI scheduling is only for large factories
  10. CTA: Link to /en/contact
  11. Internal link to ai-in-manufacturing post and what-is-finite-capacity-scheduling post (use /en/blog/what-is-finite-capacity-scheduling)
